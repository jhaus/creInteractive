Kaleidoscope

File comparison for the Mac

Use Kaleidoscope to spot the differences in text and image files. Review changes in seconds with the world's most advanced file comparison application. It works great with Git, SVN and Mercurial to complete your workflow.

Kaleidoscope is insanely fast and presents changes in text and images as clear as possible.

Versions

Subversion Client for the Mac

Versions is the pleasant way to work with Subversion on your Mac. Whether you're a Subversion power user or completely new to version control, Versions will improve your workflow. Keeping track of a project with Subversion was never this effortless.

Subversion is the world's most used open source version control system, with Versions it's now also the world's easiest to use.

Checkout

Get a Mac. Start a Store.

Checkout is the complete, helpful solution to run your retail store. Sales, orders, quotes, receipts and invoices are just the beginning. With a range of interactive reports, advanced product management and built-in label printing, Checkout helps with most facets of your business. To handle the rest, Checkout offers seamless accounting and e-commerce integration.

Checkout is the fruit of years of retail experience combined with our love for thoughtfully crafted, fun to use, software.

Enstore

Your store, online.

Enstore makes running a web store as simple as selling lemonade. We'll even squeeze the lemons for you. Sign up, sync your products, and pick your payment and shipping options. That is all there's to it. Enstore comes with a range of professionally designed templates to choose from. You can even customize the built-in templates or, if you prefer, design a unique look from scratch.

With time-saving functionality like automatic sales tax lookup and carrier shipping integration, Enstore comes with batteries included.

Design for 280 North

Cappuccino

Cappuccino is an open source framework that makes it easy to build desktop-caliber applications that run in a web browser. Sofa created its logo as the first step in a long-term collaboration with 280 North.

More information on www.cappuccino.org

Aristo

Aristo is an open source user interface that ships with Cappuccino. Sofa worked with 280 North to create an elegant set of interface elements that gives freedom to developers in terms of color, size and style. One of the key goals was to find a look and feel that works on multiple platforms.

More information: http://github.com/280north/aristo

About

Sofa is a privately held software company, founded in 2006. Since then, we've created several great products and steadily added more talent to our team.

We don't believe in rigid methodologies or in writing exhaustive specs. The best software is made through a flexible, iterative process, by small teams that ‘own’ the problem at hand.

Apple Design Award 2009Versions won an award in the Developer Showcase category. More about the ceremony at Macworld.com.

Apple Design Award 2008Checkout was rewarded runner-up for delivering a great user experience. More info at Macworld.com.

BusinessWeek AwardMadebysofa.com won a Best of the Web Award for bringing print design to the web. More info at BusinessWeek.com.

In 2007, Sofa and Acclivity joined forces in Werck, an international joint venture to further develop, market and support Checkout. Versions is the fruit of a collaboration between Sofa and Pico.

Events and Press

Whenever we can, we host or talk at events related to our work. We frequently host Amsterdam Cocoaheads meetings at our office. We're also actively involved in STIKK, a Dutch startup collective.

  • At Kings of Code in June 2009, Jorn and 280 North's Francisco Tolmasky gave a talk on Cappuccino and Aristo. Meanwhile, Jasper and Jonathan hosted a Subversion/Versions workshop.
  • At the San Francisco Cocoaheads meeting during WWDC '09, Dirk gave a presentation about bootstrapping Sofa, and Jorn presented on Cappuccino and Aristo with Francisco Tolmasky.
  • In May 2009, Koen presented at the Ruby on OS X conference to share his experience creating Mac desktop apps in interpreted languages.
  • On April 6, 2009, the Dutch newspaper Het Financieele Dagblad published an article profiling us.
  • At the WWDC '08 San Fransisco Cocoaheads meet-up, Jasper gave a presentation about combining design contract work with creating your own products, and Dirk gave a presentation about PyObjC.
  • In 2007, the Apple Developer Connection interviewed us about how we used PyObjC to create Checkout.
